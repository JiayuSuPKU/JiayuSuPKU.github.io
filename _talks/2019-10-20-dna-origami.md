Introduction: 
======
DNA, the genetic information carrier in most living organisms, has become one of the most extensively used molecular building blocks for the construction of nanoscale objects over the past three decades. DNA origami (折纸, from ori meaning "folding", and kami meaning "paper"), for instance, is the nanoscale folding of DNA to create non-arbitrary two- and three-dimensional nanostructures. DNA origami technique uses hundreds of short synthetic DNA oligonucleotides to fold a long single-stranded DNA (scaffold strand), typically M13 phage genomic DNA (~7,000 bp). Due to its high degree of customization and spatial addressability, DNA origami provides a versatile platform with which to engineer nanoscale structures and devices that can sense, compute, and actuate. These capabilities open up opportunities for a broad range of applications in chemistry, biology, physics, material science, and computer science that have often required programmed spatial control of molecules and atoms in three-dimensional (3D) space.
